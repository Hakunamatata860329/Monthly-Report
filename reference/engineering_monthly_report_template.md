# Writing Guidelines

## For Managers
Focus on:
- Impact
- Risk
- Delivery confidence
- Business value
- Resource needs

Avoid:
- Excessive implementation details

---

## For Engineers
Focus on:
- Root cause analysis
- Technical trade-offs
- Metrics
- Lessons learned
- System thinking

Avoid:
- Task-only descriptions

---

# Good Monthly Report Principles

A strong engineering report should answer:

1. What important problems were solved?
2. What measurable impact was created?
3. What risks still exist?
4. What technical decisions mattered?
5. What will happen next?

---

# Avoid These Anti-patterns

- Jira ticket dump
- Feature list without impact
- No metrics
- No risks
- No ownership
- No technical reasoning
- Too much low-level detail in summary pages

---

# Recommended Mindset

Do not write:

> “What I did this month”

Write:

> “How engineering progress moved forward this month”
